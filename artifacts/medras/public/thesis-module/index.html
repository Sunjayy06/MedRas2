Three quick questions so the editor knows what you're writing, who's writing it, and which reporting checklist to enforce.

1 · What are you writing?
2 · Which article type?
2b · What's your study design? We use this to pick the right reporting checklist (CONSORT for trials, STROBE for observational, COREQ for qualitative). It changes the chapter list in the editor.
2c · Which tier of journal are you targeting? Word counts, abstract format and reference targets all flow from this. You can change it later.
2 · Who's writing it? We use this to phrase declarations, certificates, and to pick the right title-page header (e.g. "submitted in partial fulfilment of MD…").
How the upper hand is enforced

Researcher always in control

  • Every AI suggestion appears as an inline track-change with a ✓ / ✗ — nothing auto-applies.
  • Every number from your statistical output is locked in yellow and cannot be rewritten.
  • AI never writes a sentence without an attached source from the retrieved papers.
  • Chapter / section dashboard with traffic-light progress; jump in/out in any order.
  • Helper buttons take you to the right MedRAS module (Cohort, Sigma, Novus) when you need them.
